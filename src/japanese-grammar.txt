

1. <b>Sentence Structure (Basic Word Order)</b>
Japanese is Subject-Object-Verb (SOV).

Example:
私はリンゴを食べます。
Watashi wa ringo o tabemasu.
&#8594; I eat an apple.
next

2. Particles
Particles are tiny words that mark the function of a word in a sentence:

は (wa) – topic marker
私は学生です。 – I am a student.

が (ga) – subject marker
誰が来ますか？ – Who is coming?

を (o) – direct object marker
ご飯を食べます。 – I eat rice.

に (ni) / へ (e) – direction/time marker
学校に行きます。 – I go to school.

で (de) – location of action
図書館で勉強します。 – I study at the library.

と (to) – "and" / "with"
友達と話します。 – I talk with my friend.
